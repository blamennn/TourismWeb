Our Province
Zamboanga Sibugay
Zamboanga Sibugay is a province located in the Zamboanga Peninsula region of Mindanao, Philippines. It was officially created in 2001 from the former Zamboanga del Sur through Republic Act 8973.
The province is bordered by Zamboanga del Norte to the north, Zamboanga del Sur to the east, and the Moro Gulf to the south and west. With a land area of approximately 3,636 square kilometers, it is home to a diverse and culturally rich population.
Indigenous Subanen communities, Christian settlers, and Muslim groups coexist in harmony, contributing to the province's vibrant multicultural identity.

Our Story
History of the Province
Pre-Colonial
Before 1500s
The Subanen People
The land now known as Zamboanga Sibugay was originally inhabited by the indigenous Subanen people, who developed their own rich culture, traditions, and governance long before outside contact.
Spanish Era
1500s – 1898
Colonial Influence
During Spanish rule, the region became part of the broader Zamboanga territory. Spanish missionaries influenced coastal communities while indigenous groups maintained their distinct identities inland.
American Era
1898 – 1946
Settlement & Growth
Under American administration, the area was incorporated into Zamboanga province. Christian migrants from the Visayas and Luzon began settling, shaping its demographic and cultural landscape.
2001
Republic Act 8973
A Province is Born
Zamboanga Sibugay was officially established as a separate province on November 7, 2001. Ipil was designated as the provincial capital, marking a new chapter for its people.
